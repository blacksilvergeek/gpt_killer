Author: Elizabeth Barrett Browning
Title: The Poetical Works of Elizabeth Barrett Browning, Volume IV

Yet, love, mere love, is beautiful indeed And worthy of acceptation. Fire is bright, Let temple burn, or flax; an equal light Leaps in the flame from cedar-plank or weed: And love is fire. And when I say at need _I love thee_ ... mark!... _I love thee_--in thy sight I stand transfigured, glorified aright, With conscience of the new rays that proceed Out of my face toward thine. There's nothing low In love, when love the lowest: meanest creatures Who love God, God accepts while loving so.

Author: Matthew Arnold
Title: Matthew Arnold's Sohrab and Rustum and Other Poems

So he spake, mildly; Sohrab heard his voice, The mighty voice of Rustum, and he saw His giant figure planted on the sand, Sole, like some single tower, which a chief Hath builded on the waste in former years Against the robbers; and he saw that head, Streak'd with its first grey hairs;--hope filled his soul, And he ran forward and embraced his knees, And clasp'd his hand within his own, and said:--
